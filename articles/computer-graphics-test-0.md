Introduction of Computer Graphics
Computer Graphics involves technology to access. The Process transforms and presents
information in a visual form. The role of computer graphics insensible. In today life, computer
graphics has now become a common element in user interfaces, T.V. commercial motion
pictures.
Computer Graphics is the creation of pictures with the help of a computer. The end product of
the computer graphics is a picture it may be a business graph, drawing, and engineering.
In computer graphics, two or three-dimensional pictures can be created that are used for
research. Many hardware devices algorithm has been developing for improving the speed of
picture generation with the passes of time. It includes the creation storage of models and image
of objects. These models for various fields like engineering, mathematical and so on.
Computer Graphics is the creation and manipulation of images or pictures with the help of
computers. The major product of computer graphics is a picture. With the help of CG,
pictures can be represented in 2D and 3D space. Many applications show various parts of the
displayed picture changing in size and orientation.
Application of Computer Graphics
1. Education and Training: Computer-generated model of the physical, financial and
economic system is often used as educational aids. Model of physical systems, physiological
system, population trends or equipment can help trainees to understand the operation of the
system.
For some training applications, particular systems are designed. For example Flight Simulator.
Flight Simulator: It helps in giving training to the pilots of airplanes. These pilots spend much
of their training not in a real aircraft but on the ground at the controls of a Flight Simulator.

into their structure with the help of computer graphics.
3. Computer-Generated Maps: Town planners and transportation engineers can use
computer-generated maps which display data useful to them in their planning work.
4. Architect: Architect can explore an alternative solution to design problems at an
interactive graphics terminal. In this way, they can test many more solutions that would not be
possible without the computer.UNIT-1

pie charts and other displays showing relationships between multiple parameters. Presentation

used to generate television and advertising commercial.

music videos and television shows.
8. Visualization: It is used for visualization of scientists, engineers, medical personnel,

Key characteristics of computer graphics include:
Pixel-Based Structure: Most computer images (raster/bitmap) are composed of a grid of small, colored pixels.
Vector Representations: Alternatively, images can be drawn using mathematical formulas that define shapes and lines, allowing for infinite scaling without quality loss.
Rendering: The process of converting 3D model data into a 2D image, which involves calculating lighting, textures, and shading to create realistic visuals.
Interactive Capabilities: Users can modify images, control, and manipulate objects in real-time, such as in UI design or gaming.
Animation: The ability to produce moving pictures, controlling speed and detail, known as motion dynamics.
Modeling and Visualization: Computer graphics create, represent, and visualize both 3D synthetic objects and complex data sets.
Hardware Dependence: Relies on dedicated components like frame buffers, GPU processing, and display monitors.
Dynamic Updates: The ability to change colors, shapes, and other properties of objects, known as update dynamics. 
Key Classifications of Computer Graphics:
Based on Image Composition:
Raster Graphics (Bitmaps): Composed of a grid of pixels (e.g., photographs) and are resolution-dependent, losing quality when resized.
Vector Graphics: Created using mathematical formulas to define lines, curves, and shapes, allowing for scaling without quality loss.
Based on Interaction:
Interactive Computer Graphics: Involves two-way communication between the user and the computer, allowing real-time manipulation of images (e.g., video games).
Passive Computer Graphics (Non-interactive): Pre-computed, one-way graphics, such as movies or animations, where the user cannot change the image.
Based on Dimensionality:
2D Graphics: Flat images that use 2D models.
3D Graphics: Utilize 3D models to create realistic, rendered images with depth.
Based on Application:
Computer-Aided Design (CAD): Used in engineering/architecture for design.
Presentation Graphics: Used to visualize data via charts and graphs.
Computer Art: Used for digital paintings and graphics.
Scientific Visualization: Used to analyze complex scientific data.

Flat panel displays (FPDs) have revolutionized modern technology by providing sleek, efficient, and high-performance screens for a wide range of applications. From televisions to smartphones and medical devices, FPDs are integral to everyday life.
Today, flat panel displays are a standard in the electronics market, constantly pushing the boundaries of resolution, color accuracy, and power efficiency.
What are Flat Panel Displays?
Flat panel displays are thin, lightweight, and compact electronic displays that are used in various devices such as televisions, computer monitors, smartphones, and more.
Unlike traditional cathode ray tube (CRT) displays, which use vacuum tubes and electron beams to produce images, FPDs utilize modern technology like liquid crystals, light-emitting diodes (LEDs), and organic compounds.
These displays are powered by an electrical charge and are known for their ability to provide crisp, clear images with excellent resolution and color reproduction.
Due to their slim profiles and energy efficiency, flat panel displays have become a popular choice for modern electronic devices.
Flat-Panel Devices are the devices that have less volume, weight, and power consumption compared to Cathode Ray Tube (CRT). Due to the advantages of the Flat-Panel Display, use of CRT decreased. As Flat Panel Devices are light in weights that's why they can be hang on walls and wear them on our wrist as a watch. Flat Panel Display (FPD) allow users to view data, graphics, text and images.
Types of Flat Panel Display: 

![Image 21](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/aryanbatras/100xsystems-storage/main/images/computer-graphics-test-0/computer-graphics-test-0-021.webp)

 
1. Emissive Display: 
The Emissive Display or Emitters are the devices that convert electrical energy into light energy.
Examples: Plasma Panel, LED (Light Emitting Diode), Flat CRT.
2. Non-Emissive Display: 
Non-Emissive Display or Non-Emitters are the devices that use optical effects to convert sunlight or some other source into graphic patterns.
Examples: LCD (Liquid Crystal Display)
Advantages of Flat Panel Devices:
Flat Panel Devices like LCD produces high quality digital images.
Flat Panel monitor are stylish and have very space saving design.
Flat Panel Devices consumes less power and give maximum image size in minimum space.
Flat Panel Devices use its full color display capability.
Full motion video can be viewed on Flat Panel Devices without artifacts or contrast loss.
Disadvantages of Flat Panel Devices:
They are very expensive compared to CRTs.
They have very high refresh rates.
Slow response times. 
They may be heavier and bulkier than other display types.
